CV (Curriculum Vitae)

Information, Frequently Asked Questions, and Tips on Your CV.

What is a CV?

CV is an abbreviation of the Latin words 'curriculum vitae', which mean 'life course'. A professional CV provides a summary and a good overview of someone's life.

Your CV includes your education(s), qualifications, work experience, skills, and qualities, giving potential employers a quick snapshot of your profile.

What should I include in my curriculum vitae?

Your CV should only contain information relevant to your potential employer. It must include:

  • Personal and contact details
  • Education and qualifications
  • Work experience
  • Skills and interests

What is a chronological CV?

The chronological CV lists time-based entries (like jobs and studies) in reverse order — your latest experience first.

The general order: contact info → personal summary → education → work experience → skills → languages → interests.

How to make your own CV?

You can use Word or free online resume tools. Or use our Resume Builder for a downloadable version in just 15 minutes.

Once complete, send your CV and cover letter with your job application.

Tips for creating your perfect CV

  • Only include relevant, value-adding info
  • Avoid odd hobbies or irrelevant details
  • Keep key info on the first page
  • Use bullet points for clarity
  • Prefer a chronological layout
